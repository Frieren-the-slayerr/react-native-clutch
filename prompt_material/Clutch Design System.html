for auto repair, on demand.

CLUTCH connects drivers who need a repair with mechanics who take the job — the marketplace rhythm of DoorDash and the social trust of Facebook, run through a diagnostic HUD language borrowed from the pit lane: signal-blue callouts, teal status states, and skill tiers earned on the job.

01 / STATUS CLARITY

Status is never ambiguous

Every job, every mechanic, every payout is legible at a glance — one color, one word, one number. No decoration competes with the status.

02 / EARNED REPUTATION

Trust is a progression

Mechanics grade up from Apprentice to Chief through completed jobs, not just stars. Reputation is visible, motivating, and hard to fake.

03 / SIGNAL RESTRAINT

One accent does the talking

Signal blue is spent on actions and alerts only. The rest of the interface stays quiet steel and graphite so the blue always means something.

The angular cut

Every telemetry HUD panel loses one corner to a 45° cut — it's how you knew you were looking at a readout, not a photograph. Tab Navigation

The role picked during onboarding decides the tab set — nobody toggles between them. Customers get four tabs built around a job; mechanics get three built around a shift.

Anatomy

  • Height 49pt content + safe-area inset (83pt total, Face ID devices)
  • Icon 21×21pt, 1.7pt stroke, SF Symbols where available
  • Label 9.5pt Plex Mono, uppercase, .04em tracking
  • Active Signal Blue icon + label, no background fill
  • Inactive Smoke Gray, never fully hidden — every tab stays legible at rest
  • Badge 7pt Brake Red dot, count omitted under 3 unread

Rules

Role-locked, not user-toggled — switching from customer to mechanic happens in Profile → Switch Role, not in the tab bar itself, so the bar always reflects one clear mode.

Home vs. Board — a customer's Home leads with a CTA to post a job; a mechanic's Board leads with the On Shift toggle and open jobs. Same tab position, different job.

No Post button in the bar — posting a job lives inside Home, not as a floating action, keeping the bar to pure navigation.

09 — Behavior

Motion

Motion reports status changes — it never performs for its own sake. Kept short (120–220ms) so the app feels responsive, not showy.

  • TAP FEEDBACK
    Scale to 97%, 90ms ease-out

    Every button and job card compresses slightly on press — the only feedback needed before the system confirms the action.

  • STATUS CHANGE
    Stepper dot fills left-to-right, 220ms

    When a job advances (Matched → En Route), the stepper's next dot fills with a quick blue wipe, paired with a light haptic tick.

  • JOB ACCEPTED
    Card lifts and slides out, 180ms

    On the mechanic's board, an accepted job lifts on the z-axis and slides off-list rather than fading — a physical sense of the job being picked up.

  • GRADE UP
    XP bar overshoots then settles, 400ms

    The one moment allowed to be a little louder: the XP fill briefly overshoots its target with a flash of amber before settling, paired with a success haptic.

  • MAP PIN
    Drop + settle, 260ms

    New pins drop from above with a slight bounce on landing; the mechanic's live pin eases continuously along the route rather than jumping between updates.

10 — Implementation

iOS Notes

SF Symbols first

Use SF Symbols for every standard action (chat, location, camera, checkmark). Reserve the custom set — wrench, chevron grade marks, cut-corner panel — for things Clutch alone needs.

Dynamic Type

Body and label styles map to iOS text styles (Body, Footnote, Caption) so they scale with the user's system text size. Oswald display sizes cap growth at two steps to protect card layouts.

Safe area & tab bar

Tab bar height is 49pt content + safe-area inset. Primary CTA buttons stay 16pt above the safe-area edge so they never crowd the home indicator.

Haptics

Light impact on accept/decline taps, success notification haptic on job completion and rank-up, warning haptic on cancellation. No haptic on passive status polling.

Light mode

Dark is the default identity. A light variant swaps Gunmetal Black → #F4F2EE and Steel Panel → #FFFFFF while every accent color (blue, teal, cyan, red) stays fixed for consistent status meaning.

Contrast

Fog White on Gunmetal Black exceeds 15:1. Signal Blue on Gunmetal Black sits at 5.6:1 — safe for large/bold text and icons, paired with a label rather than color alone for status.
